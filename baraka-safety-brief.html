📚 Section 5: 5 Essential Books (Read in Order)

1. "The Simple Path to Wealth" — JL Collins

Written as letters to the author's daughter. Zero jargon. Key: "The stock market transfers money from the impatient to the patient." 6 hours.

2. "The Little Book of Common Sense Investing" — John C. Bogle

By the inventor of the index fund. The bible. Key: A 1% annual fee costs 25% of returns over 30 years. 4 hours.

3. "A Random Walk Down Wall Street" — Burton Malkiel

Academic proof you can't beat the market. Key: Monkeys throwing darts match professional stock pickers. 10 hours.

4. "The Psychology of Money" — Morgan Housel

Not math — behavior. Why smart people make dumb money decisions. Key: Your experiences are 0.00000001% of world events but 80% of how you think. 6 hours.

5. "I Will Teach You To Be Rich" — Ramit Sethi

Practical automation. Exact scripts for fees, accounts, investing. Key: "Spend extravagantly on what you love, cut mercilessly on what you don't." 8 hours.
